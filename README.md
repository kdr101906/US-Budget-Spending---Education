Question: To what extent is the change in state NAEP scores in 2019, 2022, and 2024 correlated with the level of federal Department of Education funding each state recieves? 

A comparison of the highest and lowest performing states on the NAEP (National Assessment of Educational Progress) educational assessment will show that federal funding levels are not a primary predictor for NAEP scores. Scoring a higher score on the assessment is not associated with higher federal funding for Education in that state. Therefore, policy should focus on using more federal funding that is broadly distributed to strategically target lower performing states on the NAEP, where the funds are used for tutoring or literacy programs. Federal funding could also be used for programs that allow representatives from the lower performing states to visit the higher performing states so that they can adopt different teaching styles or curriculums that will maybe help their students perform better.
